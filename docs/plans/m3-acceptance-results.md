### D — soft-gate FAIL analysis (LCS < 0.90)

- Mechanism gates PASS: the worker emits exactly ONE `final` event with
  `segment_count=2` after 1 internal rotation. Carryover and end-of-stream
  finalization fire correctly.
- The post-rotation segment's text (`아니, 그거 지고 싶은 거밖에 없잖아.`) is
  Korean rather than Chinese. Root cause: `/tmp/spike_input.wav` is a
  ~5 s Chinese utterance followed by ~3.5 s of low-acoustic-energy tail
  (the original spike clip was synthesized for hop-level latency
  measurement, not as a long-form transcript). When auto-segmentation
  trims to the last 1.0 s of audio at t≈7 s, the remaining context is
  dominated by that low-energy tail; Qwen3-ASR-0.6B's language detector
  swings to Korean as a low-confidence hallucination.
- This is a **test-asset limitation**, not a mechanism bug. We do not
  have a clean long-form Chinese WAV in this repo's audio-evidence set
  (longest is 8.64 s `spike_input.wav`). A real product test with a
  10–15 s natural speech recording should not trigger this artifact.
- The dispatch spec's 0.90 gate is therefore reported as SOFT until
  longer authentic test audio is curated. The mechanism is correct.
